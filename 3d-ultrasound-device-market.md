<p><p>The 3D ultrasound device market is primarily segmented into color ultrasound and black-and-white (B/W) ultrasound technologies. Color ultrasound uses Doppler imaging to visualize blood flow and other dynamic processes, offering enhanced diagnostic capabilities in obstetrics and cardiology. In contrast, B/W ultrasound provides detailed anatomical images, primarily used for assessing structural abnormalities and guiding interventions. Both types play crucial roles in medical imaging, providing vital information for patient care but differ in their functionalities and specificity.</p></p>
