<p><p>The safety venous needle market is divided into two primary types: soft-connected and hard-connected needles. Soft-connected needles feature a flexible connection that allows for greater maneuverability and comfort during procedures, minimizing patient trauma. In contrast, hard-connected needles have a rigid attachment, providing stability and ensuring a secure connection to infusion devices. Both types prioritize safety by reducing the risk of needle-stick injuries, but their designs cater to different clinical preferences and procedural needs, influencing their market demand and applications.</p></p>
