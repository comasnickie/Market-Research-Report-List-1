<p><p>Membrane separation technology encompasses various filtration methods for separating substances in liquids. Reverse osmosis is used for removing dissolved solids, ideal for water purification. Ultrafiltration targets larger particles and macromolecules, making it suitable for wastewater treatment. Nanofiltration bridges the gap between ultrafiltration and reverse osmosis, filtering divalent ions and small organic molecules. Microfiltration focuses on separating larger particles and microorganisms, often used in food and beverage industries. Other market types may include electrodialysis and membrane distillation, expanding application versatility.</p></p>
