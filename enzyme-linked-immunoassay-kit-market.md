<p><p>The enzyme-linked immunoassay (ELISA) kit market comprises several key types. Sandwich ELISA uses two antibodies to capture and detect the target antigen, offering high specificity and sensitivity. Indirect ELISA employs a primary antibody for antigen detection, with a secondary enzyme-linked antibody to amplify the signal. Competitive ELISA measures the concentration of an antigen by comparing it to a labeled version, useful for small molecules. Other types include microplate-based assays and multiplex assays, catering to diverse research and clinical applications.</p></p>
