<p><p>The portable color ultrasound equipment market encompasses various imaging technologies: 2D, 3D, and 4D. </p><p>2D ultrasound provides two-dimensional images, primarily used for basic assessments. </p><p>3D ultrasound captures volumetric data, offering detailed images for enhanced diagnostics, especially in obstetrics. </p><p>4D ultrasound adds the dimension of time, enabling real-time visualization of moving structures, enhancing the understanding of fetal development. </p><p>Doppler ultrasound, on the other hand, evaluates blood flow and vascular health by measuring the change in frequency of ultrasound waves as they reflect off moving blood cells.</p></p>
