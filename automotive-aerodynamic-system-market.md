<p><p>The Automotive Aerodynamic System market comprises two main types: Active and Passive Systems. Active systems dynamically adjust components like spoilers, flaps, or air vents to optimize airflow and reduce drag based on driving conditions, enhancing performance and fuel efficiency. Passive systems, on the other hand, use fixed designs such as streamlined body shapes, underbody panels, or rear diffusers to improve aerodynamics without moving parts. Both systems aim to minimize drag, boost stability, and improve vehicle efficiency, catering to different performance and cost requirements.</p></p>
