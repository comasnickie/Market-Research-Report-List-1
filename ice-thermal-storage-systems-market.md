<p><p>Ice thermal storage systems are widely categorized into two main market types: ground installation and underground installation. Ground installation involves placing ice storage tanks on the surface, generally in open areas, making them more accessible for maintenance and monitoring. In contrast, underground installation involves burying the storage tanks below the ground, which helps conserve space and can enhance aesthetic appeal. Both types utilize ice to store excess cooling energy, optimizing energy efficiency in cooling applications and supporting demand-side management.</p></p>
