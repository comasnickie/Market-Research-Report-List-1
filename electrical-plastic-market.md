<p><p>The electrical plastic market is divided into halogenated and non-halogenated types based on flame retardancy. Halogenated plastics contain chlorine or bromine, offering excellent fire resistance but raising environmental and health concerns due to toxic emissions when burned. Non-halogenated plastics use alternative flame retardants like phosphorus or nitrogen, providing safer, eco-friendly solutions with lower toxicity. Both types are used in wiring, cables, and electronic components, with non-halogenated plastics gaining traction due to stricter environmental regulations and sustainability demands.</p></p>
