<p><ul><li>Based on Small Molecules</li><li>Based on Polymers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The OLED flexible display market is broadly categorized into two types: small molecules and polymers. Small molecules utilize organic compounds that can form thin films, enabling high-resolution displays with excellent color accuracy and contrast. These are prevalent in smartphones and high-end televisions. In contrast, polymer-based OLEDs use conductive plastic materials, offering flexibility and lightweight characteristics. They are increasingly used in wearables and innovative applications, providing versatile design options while maintaining durability and efficiency in various lighting conditions.</p></p>
