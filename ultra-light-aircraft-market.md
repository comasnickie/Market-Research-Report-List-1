<p><p>The ultra-light aircraft market is divided into two primary types: rotary wing and fixed wing. Rotary wing aircraft, such as helicopters, utilize rotating blades for lift, offering versatility in vertical takeoff and landing, making them ideal for short-distance travel and inaccessible areas. Fixed wing aircraft, on the other hand, rely on wings for lift and are typically more efficient for longer distances. Both types cater to recreational flying, agricultural operations, and aerial surveying, appealing to diverse user needs and preferences.</p></p>
